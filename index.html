The problem

Where trading-product firms usually go wrong.

Most clients arrive with the same four frustrations. Each one is fixable; almost none are addressed honestly elsewhere.

Issue 01

Hidden drawdown

Performance is quoted; the worst week isn't. A 6.5% maximum drawdown limit is meaningless if you don't know whether it's been hit and when.

Issue 02

"Set and forget" theatre

Systems sold as hands-off rarely are. Prop firm rule changes, broker outages, and parameter drift all need attention. Pretending otherwise hides risk.

Issue 03

Marketing dressed as research

Backtests cherry-picked to the best year. Slippage assumed at zero. Live results merged with simulation. The numbers stop meaning anything.

Issue 04

No accountability

You speak to a sales rep, then an account manager, then a support inbox. Nobody who actually runs the system is ever in the room.

The approach

How we work differently.

Three principles applied to every engagement, regardless of size or shape.

Principle 01

Verifiable, not aspirational

Every performance figure we cite is backed by a broker statement or an audited backtest. The worst week appears with the same prominence as the best. If we can't verify it, we don't claim it.

Principle 02

Production-first systems

Strategies are designed for live execution from day one. Slippage, commission, broker quirks, and rule changes are part of the build, not a footnote on a marketing page.

Principle 03

Direct accountability

You speak to the team that does the work. No salespeople, no scripted calls, no white-label sub-contracts. The people running your strategy are the same people on the discovery call.
